WELCOME TO THE OFFICIAL PAGE OF NATURAL SELECTION

STOP 1: JACKSON HOLE

Jackson Hole Mountain Resort at Teton Village, Wyoming is named after the historically significant valley and is known for its steep terrain and large continuous vertical drop of 4,139ft.

January 24-31st

STOP 2: BALDFACE LODGE

Baldface Lodge is nestled in the Kootenay Range of Selkirk Mountains at an elevation of 6,700 feet. 32,000 acres of terrain are situated just north of Nelson, British Columbia, Canada. With 500+ inches of annual snowfall to blanket its god-gladed, mid-alpine tree runs, Baldface sits atop every snowboarder's bucket list. 

February 20-27th

STOP 3: ALASKA

The Tordrillo Mountains are a small range between the Chigmit and Aleutian Ranges, 75 miles west-northwest and visible from Anchorage. Heavily glaciated and primarily volcanic, the Tords are arguably one of the best venues on the planet for riders to push the boundaries of big mountain freestyle riding. 

March 20-27th
